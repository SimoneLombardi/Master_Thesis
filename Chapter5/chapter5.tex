The test was performed with only the \textit{End effector target} task active, the following graphs show the \textit{norm} of the \textit{cartesian error} in time during the experiments, separated in position and orientation.\\

\noindent To resolve this problem I decided to add in the obstacle avoidance task, multiple links. Referencing the definition of the \textit{obstacle avoidance} task in  \ref{chap:third} the multi-link version is defined by staking the tasks in a single matrix.\\
\noindent The reference rate is a column vector composed of $n$ part, where $n$ is the number of links considered in the task.
\begin{equation}
	\vec{\dot{\bar{x}}}_{multi\_link} = 
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\vec{\dot{\bar{x}}}_{1} & \vec{\dot{\bar{x}}}_{2} & ... & \vec{\dot{\bar{x}}}_{n}
	\end{bmatrix}^T
	\hfill
	\in
	\hfill
	\mathbb{R}^{1\times (n*task\_dim)}
	\label{equat:multi_link_ref_rate}
\end{equation}
\noindent The dimension of each reference rate vector is dependent on the selected dimension for the \textit{obstacle avoidance} task, either $task\_dim = 2$ or $task\_dim = 3$.
\noindent For the \textit{activation function} we have a square matrix of size $n*task\_dim$.
\begin{equation}
	\vec{A}_{multi\_link} = 
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\vec{A}_{1} & \vec{0} &  \vec{0} \\ 
		 		\vec{0}	& ... &  		\vec{0}	  \\
		 	\vec{0}		&  \vec{0}   & \vec{A}_{n}  \\
	\end{bmatrix}
	\hfill
	\in
	\hfill
	\mathbb{R}^{(n*task\_dim)\times (n*task\_dim)}
\end{equation}
\noindent Lastly for the \textit{task jacobian} I stacked the geometric jacobian for the specific point in the robot point cloud.
\begin{equation}
	\vec{J}_{multi\_link} = 
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\vec{J}_{1}  \\ 
		\vec{J}_{2}  \\
				...	 \\
		\vec{J}_{n}  \\
	\end{bmatrix}
	\hfill
	\in
	\hfill
	\mathbb{R}^{(n*task\_dim)\times 12}
\end{equation}
\noindent This structure allows to have multiple \textit{obstacle avoidance} task at the same priority level. The \textit{proximity task} are sent from the sensing node for each link, after sorting the task in ascending order for the distance from the obstacle, the first $n$ task are taken and used to compute the control step. 
The result for the multi-target test for $3$, $6$ and $9$ links are:
